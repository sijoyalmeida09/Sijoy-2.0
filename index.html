When I was 12, my piano ran on five disposable batteries. I couldn't afford to keep replacing them. So I took a dead set-top box adapter, cut the wires, matched the voltage and polarity, and built my own power supply.

Nobody taught me how. I just needed to play.

That was my first infrastructure problem. I've been solving them ever since.

— Vasai, Maharashtra. Age 12.
Chapter 01 — The Path
From Vasai to Boston.
Every chapter built the next.
I didn't have a plan. I had a pattern: find something broken, figure out how it works, fix it, make it better.
2000 — VASAI, MAHARASHTRA
Where builders come from
Born in Vasai. Goan roots. An auto-rickshaw driver's son who ran the school funfair at 12, taught himself electronics from physics textbooks, and played church piano with a hacked power supply.
2018 — UNIVERSITY OF MUMBAI
B.E. Information Technology
Formal CS education while running an informal IT business on the side. 400+ laptop repairs. 1,000+ OS installs. 100+ SSD upgrades. By 19, charging for work he'd been doing for free — after watching a technician bill ₹500 for a job he could do better.
2019 — JOSHO IT
The company that built companies
Built the entire IT department for CLC Abroad — LAN/WAN, CCTV across 3 offices, CRM automation, WhatsApp API processing 1,000+ leads/month. The company grew from 3 to 27 employees on his infrastructure.
3 → 27 employees scaled
60% workload reduction
2020 — JOSHO MUSIC
The Vasaikar who went viral
Self-taught vocalist and audio engineer. "Vasaikar Masala" became a cultural moment in the Konkani community. Not luck — data. Reverse-engineered content algorithms and treated music distribution like a product pipeline.

2023 — NORTHEASTERN UNIVERSITY
MS Information Systems · 3.8 GPA
Cloud computing, application engineering, database design. Formalized what he'd been doing in the field for years. Graduated May 2025. Immediately kept building — 50+ SMB clients across Boston and Maine.
NOW — THE CONVERGENCE
It all connects
AI. Music tech. Real estate platforms. Full-stack systems. Infrastructure for immigrant-owned businesses. Five years of building proves it: every industry is a system, and systems are what I build.
JoSho = John + Shobha
